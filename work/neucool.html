NEU COOL

School Project

For our final Design Process, Context and Systems project, my team and I chose to improve the fundamental application structure that Northeastern University students use to apply to Co-Ops. I chose to design a mobile interface to bring our user experience modifications to life and streamline the convoluted Co-Op application processes used throughout Northeastern University.

The Old: After conducting basic research and student surveys, we discovered that each college within Northeastern utilized a different application structure for Co-Op jobs, as seen in the effort charts above. These discrepancies created considerable confusion for dual-major students, as well those students looking to apply to Co-Ops not directly related to their major or college. On the interface side, we observed an unnecessarily complicated user-flow of viewing and rating job prospects in myNEU Cool. With five slightly different bins in which students could view jobs, including “All Jobs,” “Very Interested,” “Moderately Interested,”Not Very Interested,” “Do Not Apply,” and “Not Selected”, there was a great deal of redundancy and ample room for improvement.

The New: We adopted the current College of Computer and Information Science model as the model application process for students. Rather than confuse students with differing levels of interest, we created three bins of jobs on the interface side: “Available,” “Interested,” and “Archived", the latter of which would be designated as jobs of no interest to be removed from the general listing of jobs. Users of our app would be able swipe individual jobs to mark interested or archive (see next page), batch select multiple jobs to mark interested or archived (see next page), and click on specific jobs to view the details and mark interested or archive.
